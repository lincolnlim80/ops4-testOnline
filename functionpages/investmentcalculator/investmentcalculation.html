[4] Cash on Cash (CoC)

Cash-on-cash return measures the amount of cash flow (NOI) relative to the amount of cash invested in a property investment and is calculated on a pre-tax basis.

Typical Method (first year): Focus on cash return based on first year cash flow in relation to cash committed including downpayment and acquisition costs (duties, reno/repair, etc.). The higher the better unless negative.

[5] Return on Equity (RoE)

RoE measures the income (i.e. cashflow) to net worth (equity is 'net worth' which is market value minus liabilities and includes cash committed, but ignores one-off financial costs) and is calculated on a pre-tax basis.

Method 1 (first year): Cash Flow/Cash initially committed. This is a simple metric to assess 'interest-only' loans. The higher the better.
